## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

### What We Do
DropGood is an **on-demand donation pickup platform** that eliminates the hassle of donating to charity. We're "Uber for charity donations" - customers book white-glove pickup services through our web platform, and we handle the rest: pickup from their doorstep, transport to their chosen local charity, photo confirmation, and automatic tax receipt delivery.

### The Problem We Solve
**For Donors:**
- Donating requires 2+ hours (packing, loading, driving, unloading, waiting)
- Charity drop-off centers have limited hours
- Heavy/bulky items are difficult to transport
- No convenient way to get tax receipts

**For Charities:**
- Struggle to attract donations due to donor inconvenience
- Limited staff for pickup services
- Lose potential donations to landfills/disposal

### Our Solution
A simple 3-step booking process:
1. Enter address → See exact prices for nearby charities
2. Choose charity & pickup time → Upload photos of items
3. Pay → Driver picks up & delivers → Automatic tax receipt

**Time to book:** 2 minutes
**Service coverage:** 500+ cities nationwide
**Pickup speed:** Same-day or schedule up to 7 days ahead

### Market Opportunity
**Target Market:** Affluent homeowners (35-65 years old, $150K+ income) who value time over money
**Secondary Market:** Companies offering employee wellness benefits (B2B subscription model)
**Market Size:** $15B+ charitable donations industry, growing 3-5% annually

### Competitive Advantages
✅ **Convenience:** On-demand vs. limited charity schedules
✅ **Transparent Pricing:** Distance-based, upfront costs (no surprises)
✅ **National Scale:** Uber Direct API enables 500+ city coverage instantly
✅ **Tax Compliance:** Automated receipts for all donations
✅ **Premium Positioning:** "White glove service" vs. "junk removal"

### Financial Highlights (Conservative Projections)

## BUSINESS MODEL & REVENUE STREAMS

### Revenue Stream #1: B2C Pickup Service Fees (Primary)

#### Pricing Model: Distance-Based
Our pricing is transparent and simple: **The closer the charity, the lower the price.**

**Base Pricing Formula:**
```
Uber Cost = $3.50 base + ($0.85 × distance in miles)

Customer Total Price:
├─ Delivery Fee = Uber Cost + 15% markup + 3.5% state fee* + bag/box fees
├─ Service Fee = 10% of Uber cost (shown separately)
├─ Optional Customer Tip (no minimum)
├─ Bag Fees: $2.00/bag → 100% to driver as tip
├─ Box Fees: $2.50/box → 100% to driver as tip
└─ Stripe Processing Fee: 2.9% + $0.30

*State fee applies in: CA, CO, TX, FL, GA, MS, OH, KS, NC, LA, AZ, UT, NV, WY, MI, SC
```

**Total Markup Breakdown:**
- **Active/Verified Charities:** 35% total markup (delivery + service fee)
- **Inactive/Unverified Charities:** 50% total markup (higher fee incentivizes charity verification)

**Pricing Examples (Real-World):**
| Distance | Uber Cost | Delivery Fee | Service Fee | Subtotal (before tip) | Example Total* |
|----------|-----------|--------------|-------------|----------------------|----------------|
| 3 miles  | $6.05     | $7.43        | $0.61       | $8.04                | $14-18         |
| 7 miles  | $9.45     | $11.61       | $0.95       | $12.56               | $20-25         |
| 12 miles | $13.70    | $16.83       | $1.37       | $18.20               | $28-35         |
| 20 miles | $20.50    | $25.19       | $2.05       | $27.24               | $42-50         |

*Including average 2-3 bags/boxes and Stripe fees

#### Advance Booking Discounts (Incentivize Planning)
Customers save by booking ahead (reduces same-day operational stress):
- **2-3 days ahead:** 5-10% off
- **4-5 days ahead:** 15-18% off
- **6+ days ahead:** 22-25% off (max discount)

#### Bag/Box Fee Structure (Driver Compensation)
To replace mandatory driver tips, we introduced **item-based fees**:
- **$2.00 per bag** (100% goes to driver as tip)
- **$2.50 per box** (100% goes to driver as tip)

**Why this works:**
- Drivers earn based on effort (more items = more work = more pay)
- Customers see value (transparent, effort-based pricing)
- Scales better than flat tips (fairer compensation model)

**Average pickup:** 2-4 bags/boxes → Driver earns $4-10 in tips

### Revenue Stream #2: B2B Company Sponsorships (Launching Soon)

#### Model: Corporate Sponsorship System (Like Charity Sponsorships)

**IMPORTANT:** This is NOT a subscription model. Companies work EXACTLY like donation centers with sponsorships - they're "corporate sponsors" subsidizing employee pickups.

**How It Works:**
1. **Sign up FREE** - No subscription fees, no monthly charges, EVER
2. **Add credit balance** - Companies deposit funds ($500, $1,000, $5,000, etc.)
3. **Set subsidy percentage** - Choose 0-100% employee discount
4. **Share access code** - Employees enter code during booking
5. **Auto-applied subsidies** - Company credit deducted per booking (pay only when used)

**The Brilliant Part: We Earn Full Service Fees on BOTH Portions**

Unlike charity sponsorships (where charity pays subsidy), here BOTH customer AND company pay portions of the pickup cost, but **we still earn our full 35% service fee on the original price**.

#### Revenue Example (Why This is Genius)

**Employee books a $30 pickup, company subsidizes 50%:**

**Payment Split:**
- Customer pays: **$15** (50% of total)
- Company pays: **$15** (50% subsidy from credit balance)
- Total collected: **$30**

**Our Revenue Breakdown:**
- Uber cost: $10.50 (goes to Uber)
- Driver tips (bags/boxes): $5.00 (goes to driver)
- **Our service fee: $10.50** (35% of full $30 price)
- Stripe fee: $4.00

**Why We Love B2B Transactions:**
- We earn **FULL service fees** on the original price
- Company pays subsidy (not us - it's their marketing/CSR expense)
- More transactions = more revenue (employees use benefit more)
- **B2B pickups are MORE profitable** than regular pickups (we get paid twice!)

#### Suggested Credit Tiers (Flexible - Not Subscriptions!)

| Tier | Initial Credit | Recommended Subsidy | Typical Company Size |

| **Growth** | $1,500-3,000 | 50-75% | 50-200 employees |
| **Enterprise** | $5,000+ | 75-100% | 200+ employees |

**Companies can add more credits anytime** (like donation centers refilling sponsorships).

**Value Proposition for Companies:**

## RISK ANALYSIS & MITIGATION

### Risk 1: Uber Direct API Dependency
**Risk:** If Uber changes pricing or TOS, our model breaks
**Likelihood:** Low (Uber incentivized to grow B2B API)
**Mitigation:**
- Build integrations with DoorDash Drive, Roadie (backup logistics partners)
- Negotiate volume discounts with Uber (enterprise contract)
- Develop proprietary driver network as backup (Phase 4+)

### Risk 2: Low Customer Demand
**Risk:** Not enough people want donation pickup services
**Likelihood:** Medium (unproven market demand)
**Mitigation:**
- MVP test in 1 city first (Austin - already validated)
- $500 low-risk test budget
- Pivot to B2B if B2C demand is soft (companies have budget)
- Partner with charities who have existing donor bases

### Risk 3: Charity Partnership Resistance
**Risk:** Charities don't want to partner (see us as competition)
**Likelihood:** Low (we drive MORE donations to them)
**Mitigation:**
- Educate charities on value prop (more volume)
- Offer free service for first 10 pickups (prove value)
- Share data: "We drove $X donations to you this month"
- Sign exclusive partnerships with top charities

### Risk 4: Pricing Pressure (Race to Bottom)
**Risk:** Competitors undercut prices, we lose margin
**Likelihood:** Medium (marketplace dynamics)
**Mitigation:**
- Premium positioning (we're NOT the cheapest, we're the best)
- Focus on UX, speed, convenience (not just price)
- Build brand loyalty (repeat customers less price-sensitive)
- Charity sponsorships offset customer costs (subsidies)

### Risk 5: Regulatory/Tax Law Changes
**Risk:** Tax laws change, donation deductions become less valuable
**Likelihood:** Low (charitable deductions politically popular)
**Mitigation:**
- Diversify value prop beyond tax benefits (convenience, social impact)
- Monitor tax law changes, adapt messaging
- B2B revenue less dependent on individual tax incentives
